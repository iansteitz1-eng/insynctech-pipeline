# ARIA COMPETITORS (AI Voice / Receptionist)

## Smith.ai
| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| What they do | Human + AI hybrid receptionist service |
| Pricing | $240-900/mo (per-call pricing, 20-80 calls) |
| Strengths | Established brand, human backup, CRM integrations |
| Weaknesses | Expensive, limited hours on lower tiers, human dependency = scaling issues |
| Their customers | Law firms, home services, small professional services |
| **Our angle** | "Great if you want humans. The tradeoff is 3x our cost for limited hours. Our AI is 24/7, unlimited calls, fully automated." |
| **When they win** | When client absolutely requires a human voice for sensitive conversations (legal intake, medical) |
| **When we win** | When client needs 24/7, unlimited capacity, and cost efficiency |

## Ruby Receptionists
| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| What they do | Human-only virtual receptionist service |
| Pricing | $235-640/mo (20-80 receptionist minutes) |
| Strengths | Premium human touch, strong brand, high NPS |
| Weaknesses | All-human = expensive + limited scalability + daytime hours only |
| Their customers | Law firms, financial advisors, medical practices |
| **Our angle** | "Ruby is premium but limited. $235/mo gets you 20 minutes. Our $149/mo gets you unlimited 24/7." |
| **When they win** | High-touch professional services where human warmth is non-negotiable |
| **When we win** | Volume businesses (salons, restaurants, service shops) where speed and availability > human touch |

## Bland AI
| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| What they do | API-first AI phone agent platform |
| Pricing | Pay-per-minute (developer-focused) |
| Strengths | Flexible API, developer-friendly, customizable |
| Weaknesses | Zero turnkey solution. Client must build everything. No managed service. |
| Their customers | Developers, tech companies building their own agents |
| **Our angle** | "Bland is for developers who want to build from scratch. We build, deploy, and manage — the business owner touches nothing." |
| **When they win** | When the buyer is a developer who wants full control |
| **When we win** | Every non-technical business owner (99% of our market) |

# CERTUSRODO COMPETITORS (AI Agent Security/Governance)

## Keycard ($38M raised)
| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| What they do | Agent identity and authentication |
| Pricing | Enterprise (not public) |
| Strengths | Well-funded, enterprise relationships, identity focus |
| Weaknesses | NO rollback. No transaction management. Authentication only. |
| **Our angle** | "They authenticate agents. We catch their mistakes. Authentication is step 1. What happens when an authenticated agent goes wrong? That's our domain." |
| **Moat** | They'd need to build our entire transaction engine to compete |

## Descope ($88M raised)
| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| What they do | Authentication / identity for apps and agents |
| Pricing | Free tier + enterprise |
| Strengths | Massive funding, drag-and-drop auth flows, broad platform |
| Weaknesses | General-purpose auth, not agent-specific. No rollback. No session governance. |
| **Our angle** | "Descope is Okta for apps. We're the safety net for agents. They handle 'who is this agent?' We handle 'what did this agent do and can we undo it?'" |

## Scalekit ($5.5M raised)
| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| What they do | Enterprise SSO/auth infrastructure |
| Pricing | Usage-based |
| Strengths | Developer experience, SSO focus |
| Weaknesses | SSO-focused, not agent governance. No rollback. No autonomous decision engine. |
| **Our angle** | "OAuth for agents is a feature, not a platform. We provide the full trust lifecycle: identity → session → transaction → rollback → learning." |

## Entro Security (Series A)
| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| What they do | Non-human identity (NHI) secrets management |
| Pricing | Enterprise |
| Strengths | NHI specialization, secrets lifecycle management |
| Weaknesses | Secrets management ≠ transaction safety. No rollback. No real-time governance. |
| **Our angle** | "Entro manages secrets. We manage actions. Knowing an agent's API key is secure doesn't help when the agent makes a bad decision with that key." |

## Oasis Security (Series B)
| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| What they do | NHI lifecycle management |
| Pricing | Enterprise |
| Strengths | Comprehensive NHI visibility, compliance features |
| Weaknesses | Lifecycle management without rollback = monitoring, not governance. |
| **Our angle** | "They watch. We act. Monitoring tells you something went wrong. Rollback fixes it." |

# COMPETITIVE RULES OF ENGAGEMENT

1. **Never name competitors in public content** — always frame as "other solutions" or "what we've seen in the market"
2. **In sales conversations:** Ask "who else are you evaluating?" then use the relevant battle card
3. **Never lie about competitors** — if they're good at something, acknowledge it, then differentiate
4. **Always bring it back to the demo** — "The best comparison is calling our demo line yourself"
5. **If a prospect chooses a competitor:** Be gracious. "Totally understand. If it doesn't work out, we're here."
6. **Update these cards quarterly** — competitors evolve, so must our positioning
