## When Does ARIA Decide to Transfer?

ARIA doesn't transfer randomly. She has specific triggers:

### Hard Triggers (Always Transfer)
| Trigger | Detection | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Caller requests human | Keywords: "real person," "manager," "speak to someone," "human" | "Can I talk to someone who works there?" |
| Emergency / urgent safety | Keywords: "emergency," "hurt," "bleeding," "fire," "danger" | "My dog ate something toxic" (vet) |
| Billing dispute | Keywords: "charge," "refund," "overcharged," "billing error" | "You charged me twice last month" |
| Legal threat | Keywords: "lawyer," "sue," "legal action," "attorney" | "I'm going to contact my lawyer" |
| Emotional distress | Sentiment analysis: anger > 0.8, distress > 0.7 | Caller is crying, yelling, or in crisis |
| Complex medical/health | PHI or clinical questions beyond scope | "What medications interact with my prescription?" |
| Complaint escalation | Caller explicitly unsatisfied with AI response | "That's not what I asked. Let me talk to the owner." |

### Soft Triggers (Transfer If Confidence < 70%)
| Trigger | Detection | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Question not in training data | ARIA doesn't know the answer after 2 attempts | "Do you offer financing on procedures over $5,000?" |
| Custom pricing request | Pricing outside standard tiers | "I need a quote for 12 locations" |
| Appointment conflict | Scheduling issue ARIA can't resolve | "I need to reschedule but only Tuesdays work and you said no Tuesdays" |
| Multi-step request | Complex chain ARIA can't fully handle | "I need to cancel, rebook, and change my insurance on file" |
| Repeat caller with unresolved issue | Same number called 2+ times same day | System detects repeat caller, prior issue unresolved |

### Never Transfer (ARIA Handles Fully)
- Basic hours/location/directions questions
- Service menu and pricing inquiries
- Simple appointment booking
- "Are you open?" / "What time do you close?"
- General information that's in the training data
- Voicemail/message taking when business is closed

## METHOD A: WHISPER BRIDGE (Premium — Best Experience)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

### What the Caller Experiences
```
ARIA: "I want to make sure you get the best help with this.
Let me connect you with [Name/role]. Just one moment."

[Caller hears: 3-5 seconds of hold music or gentle tone]

[Human answers] "Hi [Caller Name], I understand you're calling
about [topic]. How can I help?"

Caller thinks: "Wow, they already knew why I was calling."
```

### What the Human Experiences
```
[Phone rings — caller ID shows "ARIA TRANSFER" or the business number]

[Human answers]

ARIA (whisper, caller can't hear):
"Transferring Maria. Apartment inquiry. Two-bedroom with a dog,
sixty pounds. March move-in. Interested in covered parking.
Comparing three properties. She's friendly but in a hurry."

[3-second pause]

[Call bridges — human and caller connected]
```

### Twilio Implementation (Technical)

```python
# When ARIA decides to escalate:

from twilio.rest import Client

def warm_transfer_whisper(
    caller_sid,           # Current call SID
    business_owner_phone, # Number to transfer to
    context_brief_text,   # Generated by Claude
    aria_voice_id         # ElevenLabs voice for whisper
):
    client = Client(TWILIO_SID, TWILIO_AUTH)

    # Step 1: Place caller on hold
    client.calls(caller_sid).update(
        twiml='<Response><Play loop="0">hold_music.mp3</Play></Response>'
    )

    # Step 2: Call the business owner
    transfer_call = client.calls.create(
        to=business_owner_phone,
        from_=BUSINESS_NUMBER,
        twiml=f'''
        <Response>
            <Say voice="alice">ARIA transfer. Here is your brief.</Say>
            <Pause length="1"/>
            <Say voice="alice">{context_brief_text}</Say>
            <Pause length="2"/>
            <Say voice="alice">Connecting caller now.</Say>
            <Dial>
                <Conference>transfer_{caller_sid}</Conference>
            </Dial>
        </Response>
        '''
    )

    # Step 3: Once human is in conference, bridge caller in
    # (Triggered by status callback when human joins conference)
    client.calls(caller_sid).update(
        twiml=f'''
        <Response>
            <Say voice="alice">Connecting you now.</Say>
            <Dial>
                <Conference>transfer_{caller_sid}</Conference>
            </Dial>
        </Response>
        '''
    )
```

### Enhanced: Use ElevenLabs Voice for Whisper
Instead of Twilio's robotic `<Say>` voice, route the whisper through
ElevenLabs so the human hears ARIA's actual voice delivering the brief.
This creates consistency — the human recognizes "that's our AI" immediately.

```python
# Generate whisper audio via ElevenLabs
whisper_audio = elevenlabs_tts(
    text=context_brief_text,
    voice_id=ARIA_VOICE_ID,
    speed=1.3  # Slightly faster for efficiency
)
# Save to accessible URL
whisper_url = upload_to_storage(whisper_audio)

# Use <Play> instead of <Say> in Twilio TwiML
twiml = f'''
<Response>
    <Play>{whisper_url}</Play>
    <Pause length="2"/>
    <Dial><Conference>transfer_{caller_sid}</Conference></Dial>
</Response>
'''
```

### Timing
- Caller on hold: 8-15 seconds total
- Whisper to human: 5-10 seconds
- Bridge connection: 2-3 seconds
- Total transfer time: Under 20 seconds

### Failure Modes
| Scenario | Handling |
|----------|---------|
| Human doesn't answer | ARIA returns to caller: "I wasn't able to reach [Name] right now. Can I take a message and have them call you back within [timeframe]?" |
| Human's line is busy | Same as above. Take message. |
| Human answers but hangs up during whisper | ARIA returns to caller with message option. Flag to Ian. |
| Transfer takes >30 seconds | ARIA checks in with caller: "Still connecting — just another moment." |
| Twilio API error | Fall back to Method B (SMS brief). |

## METHOD B: SMS BRIEF + TRANSFER (Simple — Build First)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

### What Happens
```
1. ARIA decides to transfer
2. ARIA to caller: "Let me connect you with [Name]. One moment."
3. SIMULTANEOUSLY:
   a) ARIA sends SMS to business owner's phone:
      "ARIA TRANSFER: Maria calling about 2BR apt, has 60lb dog,
      March move-in, wants covered parking. Comparing 3 properties.
      Friendly but in a hurry. Transferring now."
   b) Twilio transfers the call to the business owner's number
4. Owner sees SMS → answers phone with context
```

### Implementation
```python
def warm_transfer_sms(
    caller_sid,
    business_owner_phone,
    context_brief_text
):
    client = Client(TWILIO_SID, TWILIO_AUTH)

    # Step 1: Send SMS brief
    client.messages.create(
        to=business_owner_phone,
        from_=BUSINESS_NUMBER,
        body=f"🔄 ARIA TRANSFER:\n{context_brief_text}\n\nIncoming call now."
    )

    # Step 2: Transfer the call (slight delay to let SMS arrive)
    import time
    time.sleep(2)  # 2-second delay for SMS delivery

    # Step 3: Forward the call
    client.calls(caller_sid).update(
        twiml=f'''
        <Response>
            <Say voice="alice">Connecting you now. Thank you for your patience.</Say>
            <Dial callerId="{BUSINESS_NUMBER}">
                <Number>{business_owner_phone}</Number>
            </Dial>
        </Response>
        '''
    )
```

### Advantages
- Buildable in one day
- Works with existing Twilio setup
- SMS arrives before or as phone rings
- Human has a text record they can reference during the call
- No complex conference bridge needed

### Limitations
- Human might not read SMS before answering (2-3 seconds window)
- Requires A2P 10DLC for SMS (⚠️ pending for InSync)
- Less polished than whisper bridge

### Workaround for A2P Pending
Until A2P clears, use WhatsApp Business API or email instead of SMS:
```python
# WhatsApp fallback via Twilio
client.messages.create(
    to=f"whatsapp:{business_owner_phone}",
    from_="whatsapp:+14155238886",  # Twilio sandbox or registered number
    body=f"🔄 ARIA TRANSFER:\n{context_brief_text}"
)
```

## METHOD E: CONFERENCE + LIVE ASSIST (Premium Enterprise)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

### What Happens
```
1. ARIA decides this is a complex or high-value call
2. ARIA to caller: "Let me bring in a specialist. One moment."
3. ARIA opens a three-way conference:
   - Caller
   - Business owner / specialist
   - ARIA (stays on the line, muted)
4. ARIA delivers the whisper brief to the human
5. Human takes over the conversation
6. ARIA listens silently, ready to:
   - Pull up information if human asks ("ARIA, what's our availability Tuesday?")
   - Take notes in real-time
   - Provide post-call summary that includes BOTH the AI and human portions
7. When call ends, ARIA generates a COMPLETE summary of the entire interaction
```

### Why This Is Enterprise-Grade
- The human is never alone — ARIA is a silent copilot
- If the human gets stuck on a fact, ARIA can interject with the answer
- The post-call summary includes everything from both the AI and human portions
- This is the "Iron Man + JARVIS" experience for business owners

### Technical Requirements
- Twilio Conference with muted participant
- Real-time speech-to-text for ARIA to "hear" the human portion
- Trigger word for human to invoke ARIA mid-call ("ARIA, check the schedule")
- Complex but achievable with Twilio + ElevenLabs + Deepgram/Whisper

## How ARIA Generates the Perfect Brief

### The Claude Prompt (Real-Time, During the Call)

```python
BRIEF_SYSTEM_PROMPT = """
You are generating a spoken context brief for a business owner
who is about to take over a phone call from an AI assistant.

The brief must:
1. Be under 40 words (5-10 seconds spoken)
2. Start with the caller's name (if given)
3. State the core reason for the call in one phrase
4. Include 2-3 key details the human needs immediately
5. Note the caller's emotional state in 2-3 words
6. Use natural spoken language (not a report)

Format: "[Name]. [Reason]. [Detail 1]. [Detail 2]. [Emotional state]."

Examples:
- "Maria. Apartment inquiry, two-bedroom. Has a large dog, sixty pounds. Needs March move-in, wants covered parking. Friendly but comparing multiple places."
- "James. Wants to reschedule his cleaning from Thursday to next week. Has Delta Dental insurance. Mentioned he's anxious about a procedure. Calm, polite."
- "Unknown caller. Oil change for a 2019 Camry, asking about synthetic. Available this afternoon only. Seems in a rush."
- "Lisa. Upset about a billing charge, says she was charged twice in February. Requesting a callback from the manager. Frustrated but not hostile."
"""

BRIEF_USER_PROMPT = f"""
Generate a context brief from this call transcript:

{transcript_so_far}

Remember: under 40 words, spoken naturally, include emotional state.
"""
```

### Brief Quality Levels

**Level 1: Basic (Available Immediately)**
- Caller's name (if stated)
- Topic of the call (1 phrase)
- Emotional state (1-2 words)
- Example: "Sarah. Appointment scheduling. Friendly."

**Level 2: Standard (After 30+ Seconds of Conversation)**
- Everything in Level 1
- 2-3 specific details mentioned
- What the caller is expecting
- Example: "Sarah. Wants a Thursday cleaning. Has Aetna. Prefers mornings. Expecting to book today."

**Level 3: Rich (After 60+ Seconds)**
- Everything in Level 2
- Decision factors or comparisons
- Objections or concerns mentioned
- Hidden needs detected
- Example: "Sarah. Cleaning plus consultation about veneers. Has Aetna PPO. Comparing your office with Smile Design across town. Main concern is cost. Mentioned she's nervous about cosmetic procedures. Warm and engaged."

### Brief Adaptation Per Vertical

The brief template adjusts based on the client's business type:

**Dental:**
```
"[Name]. [Procedure type]. [Insurance: type/none]. [Availability preference].
[Special concerns: anxiety, pain, cosmetic]. [Emotional state]."
```

**Veterinary:**
```
"[Name]. [Pet type + name if given]. [Reason: wellness/emergency/symptom].
[Pet age/breed if mentioned]. [Urgency level]. [Emotional state]."
```

**Property Management:**
```
"[Name]. [Unit type seeking]. [Move-in timeline]. [Special requirements:
pets/parking/accessibility]. [Budget if mentioned]. [Emotional state]."
```

**Auto Dealership:**
```
"[Name]. [Vehicle: year/make/model]. [Service needed].
[Timeline: urgent/flexible]. [Warranty status if mentioned]. [Emotional state]."
```

**Barbershop/Salon:**
```
"[Name]. [Service requested]. [Stylist preference if any].
[Time preference]. [Walk-in or appointment]. [Emotional state]."
```

### Real-Time Transcript Processing

ARIA doesn't wait until the transfer to generate the brief.
She builds it CONTINUOUSLY as the conversation progresses:

```
[Call second 0-15]  ARIA: Greets caller, learns name
                    Brief draft: "Maria. Unknown reason."

[Call second 15-30] Caller: Explains they need an apartment
                    Brief draft: "Maria. Apartment inquiry."

[Call second 30-60] Conversation continues, details emerge
                    Brief draft: "Maria. Two-bedroom apartment.
                    Has a sixty-pound lab. March move-in."

[Call second 60-90] More details
                    Brief draft: "Maria. Two-bedroom, large dog,
                    March move-in, wants covered parking.
                    Comparing three properties. Friendly, in a hurry."

[Transfer triggered] → Brief is READY. No generation delay.
```

This means the brief is available INSTANTLY when ARIA decides to transfer.
Zero wait time for brief generation. The caller never notices a delay.

## What Aria Does

### Monitoring
- Track transfer rate per client per week (how often does ARIA transfer?)
- If transfer rate >30%: agent needs more training data (too many unknowns)
- If transfer rate <5%: agent might be handling things it shouldn't
- Alert Ian: "Mel transferred 12 times this week, up from 4. Top reason: pricing questions not in training data. Recommend updating Mel's system prompt with current pricing."

### Optimization
- Review transcripts of transferred calls weekly
- Identify patterns: what questions cause transfers?
- Recommend system prompt updates to reduce unnecessary transfers
- "40% of Mel's transfers were about parking availability. Adding parking info to Mel's prompt would eliminate most of these."

### Reporting
- Include transfer metrics in client monthly reports
- "Your agent handled 89% of calls autonomously. 11% were transferred to you with context briefs. Top transfer reasons: [list]."

### Quality Assurance
- Spot-check brief accuracy: does the brief match the transcript?
- Track caller satisfaction after transfers (if surveys are implemented)
- Flag any transfer where the human didn't answer (missed opportunity)
